Bootstrap Grids

Bootstrap grids will become every designers' best friend and they are arguably one of Bootstrap's biggest selling points. With one line of code, you have a functional, responsive grid that adjusts to every screen size seamlessly. Bootstrap grids use a 12-grid system.

Shrink and expand your browser size to see Bootstrap Grids at work.

I'm .col-sm-4 so I take up 4 of 12 spaces and I breakdown at 576px
If you added another column, we'd take up the whole page
I'm .col-md-6 so I take up half the spaces and breakdown at 768px
There is no room to add another column
You can mix and match what sizes you want your columns to be by setting the col-#
I'm .col-lg-auto so I take up as much space as I need and breadown at 992px
No matter what, we will break down into a single column layout when the browser hits our breakpoints determined by the sm, md, lg, or xl tags.

Bootstrap Cards

Cards are content containers that are both flexible and extensible. There are a lot of things you can add to a card, such as photos, headers, footers, background colours, blockquotes, and many more elements. Fun fact: cards replaced Bootstrap Panels, Wells, and Thumbnails when we switched from Bootstrap3 to Bootstrap4.

Why Use Cards?

Bootstrap cards are great for showcasing little tidbits of information, and they're easy to use too! Just use a class of card to get started. You can add links or buttons if you want to send the user somewhere else on your site.
